    Trading & Economy Guide

    Making Gold in World of Sea Battle

    Trading is the best way to make gold in World of Sea Battle. While combat and quests provide steady income, strategic trading between north and south ports can generate millions of gold with the right commodities and routes.

    This guide covers trading fundamentals, early game gold farming through NPC ship sinking, and resource trading strategies to maximize your profits.

    Trading Basics: North ‚Üî South Routes

    The Golden Rule of Trading

    North Ports ‚Üí South Ports

    Goods available in northern ports are NOT available in southern ports, and vice versa. This creates the arbitrage opportunity for profit.

    Step 1: Buy commodities in North ports (Gray Water, Freedom Bay, etc.)

    Step 2: Sail to South ports and sell for profit

    Step 3: Buy southern commodities while you're there

    Step 4: Return to north and sell the southern goods

    ‚ö†Ô∏è Do NOT fast travel back empty! Always buy goods in the south to sell in the north on your return trip.

    Best Commodities to Trade (Priority Order)

    Best

    1. Sugar

    Highest profit-per-weight ratio. Always prioritize buying sugar first if available.

    Best

    2. Salt

    Second-best profit-per-weight. Lightweight and extremely profitable.

    Good

    3. Wine

    Solid profit margins. Buy if sugar and salt are sold out.

    Good

    4. Grog

    OK

    Weight-to-Profit Optimization
    Focus on profit per weight unit, not just total profit. Sugar and salt give you the most gold per ton of cargo space, allowing you to maximize earnings even with smaller ships.

    North vs South Port Locations

    North Ports

    Upper region of the map

    Examples: Gray Water, Freedom Bay, and other northern settlements.

    What to Buy Here:

    Check Trading House Market for Sugar, Salt, Wine, Grog, and Beer. Prioritize Sugar and Salt.

    South Ports

    Lower region of the map

    Southern settlements and ports in the bottom half of the world map.

    What to Buy Here:

    Different commodities from the north. Buy here, sell in north on your return journey.

    Ignore Middle Ports: Ports in the center of the map don't participate in north-south arbitrage. Focus on the extreme north and extreme south for maximum profit margins.

    Faction Discounts for Resource Trading

    25% Savings on Ship Building

    Each ship has a faction flag associated with it. If you build that ship at a port controlled by the matching faction, you get 25% discount on all resources.

    How It Works:

    1. 1. Click on the ship you want to build (in ship menu)
    2. 2. Look at the faction flag icon on the ship's info page
    3. 3. Open the world map and find ports with that faction's flag
    4. 4. Transport all required resources to that faction's port
    5. 5. Build the ship and save 25% on iron, wood, etc.
    Example: If Blackwind requires 500 iron, building it at a faction discount port only costs you 375 iron. That's 125 iron saved ‚Äî enough to craft multiple other items.

    Advanced Trading Tips

    Calculate Before Buying

    Don't blindly buy everything. Calculate profit margins and weight capacity to maximize each trip.

    Never Travel Empty

    Always buy goods in the destination port before returning. Empty cargo space = wasted profit.

    Upgrade to Transport Ships

    Larger cargo holds = bigger profits. Invest in Russia or higher-tier transport ships when possible.

    Avoid PvP Zones When Loaded

    Carrying valuable cargo? Take safer routes or travel with escorts to avoid losing everything to pirates.

    Sell at Peak Demand

    Some ports have higher demand for certain goods. Experiment to find the most profitable selling locations.

    Reinvest Your Profits

    Use trading gold to buy better ships, unlock more mines, and expand your trading capacity.
