    Ship Capture & Boarding Mechanics

    Boarding & Capturing Ships

    Capturing enemy ships through boarding is a core mechanic in World of Sea Battle. Successfully boarding allows you to add ships to your squadron, collect captives for manpower, or dismantle captured vessels for valuable resources.

    This guide covers boarding mechanics, the captive system, manpower usage, and ship dismantling strategies for maximum efficiency.

    How to Board Enemy Ships

    Step-by-Step Boarding Process

    Step 1

    Reduce Enemy HP Below 50%

    You can only board ships when their HP is below 50%. Fire cannons at the enemy ship until you see their health bar drop to half or less.

    Step 2

    Inspect Enemy Crew Count

    Press

    E
    while near the enemy ship to see their crew count (displayed in top-left corner, e.g., "110 crew"). Compare this to your own crew count shown in the bottom-left.

    Step 3

    Close Distance & Spam Spacebar

    Sail close to the enemy ship (almost touching). Spam

    Spacebar
    rapidly to initiate the boarding action. A boarding fight will begin automatically.

    Step 4

    Win the Boarding Fight

    The boarding fight is automatic. If you have more crew members than the enemy, you win. The ship is now yours to capture.

    CRITICAL: Crew Count Requirement

    You MUST have more crew members than the enemy ship to win boarding fights. If the enemy has 110 crew and you only have 72, you will lose the fight and your ship will sink.

    Always check enemy crew count by pressing

    E
    before attempting to board. Only board ships with fewer crew than your own.

    Understanding Crew Counts

    Your Crew Count

    Your ship's current crew is shown in the bottom-left UI (e.g., "72 crew members"). This number must be higher than the enemy's crew to board successfully.

    Captives & Manpower System

    Unlocking the Captive System

    The Captive system requires the Slave Holding skill, which unlocks at Level 15. This should be one of your priority skills after Fast Travel and Escort.

    What Are Captives?

    When you board an enemy ship, instead of inviting it to your squadron, you can take captives. These captives become manpower on your Personal Island, which accelerates crafting and building.

    How to Collect Captives:

    1. 1. Board an enemy ship (HP below 50%, more crew than them)
    2. 2. After winning the boarding fight, choose "Take Captives" instead of "Invite to Squadron"
    3. 3. The captives are added to your ship's cargo
    4. 4. Sail to your Personal Island
    5. 5. Dock at your island and click "Disembark Captives"
    6. 6. Captives are converted to manpower for island production

    Using Manpower on Your Island

    Manpower (shown as a number in top-left of your island, e.g., "127 manpower") is used to:

    Crafting Resources

    Crafting at your island workshop consumes manpower to speed up production. More manpower = faster crafting.

    Manpower Ratio: 1 captive = 1 manpower. Collect hundreds of captives by repeatedly boarding NPC ships to build up a large manpower reserve.

    Ship Dismantling for Resources

    Why Dismantle Captured Ships?

    Dismantling captured ships is one of the best ways to get Beams in the early game. It's much more efficient than crafting Beams yourself from wood and iron.

    Step-by-Step Dismantling Process:

    1. 1. Capture an enemy ship through boarding (HP below 50%, more crew than them)
    2. 2. Invite the ship to your squadron (instead of taking captives)
    3. 3. Sail to any port and dock
    4. 4. Click on the captured ship's name (shown below your main ship in the port UI)
    5. 5. Empty the ship's cargo hold â€” Move all items to your inventory or warehouse first!
    6. 6. Click the ship's name again â†’ Select "Dismantle"
    7. 7. Receive Beams, wood, iron, and other crafting materials
    WARNING: Empty Cargo First!
    Always empty the ship's cargo hold before dismantling. If you dismantle with items still in the hold, you will lose all those items permanently.

    Boarding Strategy & Tips

    Always Inspect First

    Press
    E
    to see enemy crew count and cargo. Never board blindly â€” you might lose your ship.

    Maximize Crew Capacity

    Keep your ship fully crewed at all times. Recruit at ports or use your island's Tavern to hire more crew.

    Target Weak Ships Early Game

    When starting out, only board small ships with low crew counts. Avoid battleships until you have 100+ crew.

    Captives vs. Squadron

    Take captives if you need manpower for island crafting. Invite to squadron if you want to dismantle for Beams.

    Farm NPC Ships Systematically

    Patrol shipping lanes and board every NPC ship you encounter. This builds up massive resource stockpiles over time.

    Use Boarding for Leveling

    Capturing ships grants experience. Combine boarding with sinking NPCs for efficient leveling while farming resources.
