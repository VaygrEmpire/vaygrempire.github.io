    Factions

    Overview

    Factions are the core political entities in World of Sea Battle, determining territorial control, port ownership, and guild warfare across the Archipelago. Every port and player belongs to a faction ‚Äî or chooses to remain neutral. Guild membership determines your faction affiliation, and faction warfare shapes the player-driven economy and large-scale PvP conflicts.

    The faction system creates strategic depth through port conquest, economic competition, and territorial guild wars. Your allegiance (or neutrality) defines your role in the world's power dynamics.

    Faction Types

    Military Factions

    Three military factions compete for territorial dominance through warfare and port conquest. These factions are at war with each other and can capture most ports through military operations.

    Espanyol

    Military Power

    A military faction focused on conquest and territorial expansion. Fights against Antilla and Kai and Sevria.

    Antilla

    Military Power

    A military faction competing for control of the Archipelago. At war with Espanyol and Kai and Sevria.

    Kai and Sevria

    Military Power

    A military faction vying for supremacy. Hostile to both Espanyol and Antilla in the three-way conflict.

    Military Faction Characteristics

    • ‚Ä¢ All three military factions are hostile to each other
    • ‚Ä¢ Can capture most ports through warfare
    • ‚Ä¢ Guild-based faction membership
    • ‚Ä¢ Players have individual reputation with each military faction
    • ‚Ä¢ Focus on territorial conquest and naval supremacy

    Trade Faction - The Caliphate

    The Caliphate

    Economic Power

    A trade-focused faction that remains neutral to all factions except pirates. The Caliphate controls exclusive ports that cannot be captured through military conquest ‚Äî instead, these ports are awarded through a tender system where guilds competitively bid for control.

    Caliphate Exclusive Ports (Tender System)

    • ‚Ä¢ Al-Khalifa - Cannot be conquered, awarded via tender
    • ‚Ä¢ Sharhat - Cannot be conquered, awarded via tender
    • ‚Ä¢ Assab - Cannot be conquered, awarded via tender

    These ports are awarded to Caliphate guilds through competitive economic bidding, not military force.

    Trade Faction Features

    • ‚Ä¢ Neutral to military factions (hostile only to pirates)
    • ‚Ä¢ Economic-focused gameplay
    • ‚Ä¢ Tender system for port control (bidding, not conquest)
    • ‚Ä¢ Guild membership determines faction affiliation

    Pirate Faction

    Pirates

    Lawless Freedom

    The Pirate Faction is hostile to all other factions ‚Äî military and trade alike. Pirates can capture regular ports through warfare and control exclusive Pirate Coves that only pirate guilds can own.

    Pirate Coves (Pirate-Only Locations)

    • ‚Ä¢ Filibuster Bay - Pirate faction exclusive
    • ‚Ä¢ Corsa Noire Bay - Pirate faction exclusive
    • ‚Ä¢ Naabad Fortress - Pirate faction exclusive
    • ‚Ä¢ Pirate City - Pirate faction exclusive
    • ‚Ä¢ Tortuga - Pirate faction exclusive

    Special battle mode for Pirate Cove capture (not available in Season 1).

    Pirate Faction Features

    • ‚Ä¢ Hostile to all factions (military and trade)
    • ‚Ä¢ Can capture regular ports through warfare
    • ‚Ä¢ Exclusive access to Pirate Coves
    • ‚Ä¢ Outside the law - freedom-focused gameplay
    • ‚Ä¢ Guild membership determines faction affiliation

    Neutral Status

    Neutral Players

    No Guild Affiliation

    Players who are not members of any guild are neutral. Neutral players do not belong to any faction and their standing is determined by their individual reputation with each of the three military factions.

    Neutral Player Features

    • ‚Ä¢ No faction allegiance (no guild membership)
    • ‚Ä¢ Individual reputation with each military faction
    • ‚Ä¢ More flexible interactions across factions
    • ‚Ä¢ Cannot participate in faction warfare
    • ‚Ä¢ No access to faction-specific benefits or territories

    Port Control System

    Ports in World of Sea Battle are categorized by their ownership rules and capture mechanics. Understanding port types is crucial for faction strategy.

    The Empire (NPC Faction)

    The Empire

    Non-Playable Antagonist State

    The Empire is not a playable faction ‚Äî it is an NPC state similar to China in the game world. The Empire is fighting to reclaim the Archipelago and controls most "free ports" by default until they are captured by player factions.

    Empire Characteristics

    • ‚Ä¢ Not playable - NPC antagonist state
    • ‚Ä¢ Controls "free ports" by default
    • ‚Ä¢ Fighting to reclaim the Archipelago
    • ‚Ä¢ Advanced shipbuilding technology (similar to Chinese)
    • ‚Ä¢ Unknown government structure (possibly dynastic monarchy)
    • ‚Ä¢ Unknown capital location
    • ‚Ä¢ Common enemy for all player factions

    Players fight The Empire to capture free ports and expand their faction's territory. Most ports start under Empire control and become available for faction conquest.

    Guild System & Faction Membership

    Faction affiliation is determined by guild membership. When you join a guild, you automatically join that guild's faction. All members of a guild share the same faction allegiance.

    Guild-Based Factions

    • ‚Ä¢ Guilds align with one faction
    • ‚Ä¢ All guild members join that faction
    • ‚Ä¢ Faction warfare is guild-based territorial conflict
    • ‚Ä¢ Changing guilds changes your faction
    • ‚Ä¢ Guilds can be military, trade, or pirate-aligned

    No Guild = Neutral

    • ‚Ä¢ Players without guilds are neutral
    • ‚Ä¢ No faction allegiance or warfare participation
    • ‚Ä¢ Individual reputation with each faction
    • ‚Ä¢ More flexible but lack faction benefits
    • ‚Ä¢ Cannot control ports or participate in sieges

    Reputation System

    Individual Faction Reputation

    Your standing with each military faction

    Players have individual reputation with each of the three military factions (Espanyol, Antilla, Kai and Sevria). Reputation affects your interactions with faction-controlled ports and influences how faction NPCs treat you.

    Reputation Features

    • ‚Ä¢ Separate reputation with each military faction
    • ‚Ä¢ Affects access to faction-controlled ports
    • ‚Ä¢ Determines NPC faction interactions
    • ‚Ä¢ Neutral players manage reputation with all three military factions
    • ‚Ä¢ Guild members inherit guild faction's standing

    Neutral players have the flexibility to build (or destroy) reputation with each faction independently, while guild members' faction reputation is tied to their guild's allegiance and warfare activities.
